# Hospitality Knowledge

A researched base for hotel, resort, event, and tourism content in Nepal. Built from real properties and real guest reviews, not opinion. Refresh it through research before pitching, since properties, rooms, and competitors change. No em dashes, ever.

## What guests in Nepal actually value

In priority order, synthesized from guest reviews.

- **Service that feels personal.** Guests name individual staff in reviews constantly. Warm, attentive, remembered-by-name service is the single most praised thing. Lead with people and care, not marble.
- **Cleanliness and well-kept rooms.** Spotless earns rave reviews. "Dated" is the most common complaint, even at five-star properties.
- **Food, especially breakfast.** Quality and variety come up everywhere. Welcome drinks, cold towels on arrival, and a manager's reception land hard.
- **A sense of calm and arrival.** Peaceful, a soft landing, the city falling away. A selling point in its own right.
- **The setting.** Mountains, lake, river, jungle. In Nepal the landscape is the product. Sell the view and the place as much as the room.
- **Smooth logistics.** Airport pickup, luggage storage, fast wifi, easy check-in. For trekkers, post-trek recovery and early breakfasts matter.
- **Trust and consistency.** Repeat guests return because the experience holds up. Reliability is a real differentiator against newer properties.
- **Pain points to design around:** dated rooms, noise from large tour groups, and inconsistent service at newer properties. Good messaging quietly reassures on these.

## The Soaltee portfolio, the home brand

- **The Soaltee Kathmandu, Autograph Collection.** The flagship. A long-established five-star in the capital, quieter west side, known for space, wellness, and a calm setting. Good for families and longer stays.
- **Soaltee Westend Premier, Nepalgunj.** Business-first five-star near the airport, around 81 rooms, a large banquet hall (about 4000 square feet), meeting rooms, and the only live casino in Nepalgunj. First LEED-certified green hotel in Nepal. The clear regional leader for business and events.
- **Soaltee Westend Resort, Chitwan.** A five-star jungle resort near Chitwan National Park, calm and well kept, multiple restaurants, pool and spa, strong reviews. Works for safari stays, weddings, corporate retreats, and getaways.
- **Plus** Soaltee Westend Resort Nagarkot (Himalaya-view hill retreat), Soaltee Westend Itahari in Sunsari (the eastern hub), and a Bhairahawa presence.
- **Positioning:** a homegrown Nepali five-star group spanning the capital, the western business hub, the jungle, the hills, and the east. Its edge is local roots paired with five-star service. Lean into the Nepali identity rather than imitating an international chain.

## The competitive map by city

| City | What the market is about | Key competitors | What wins here |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kathmandu | capital, business and culture | The Dwarika's (Newari heritage), Yak and Yeti (palace heritage), Hyatt Regency (resort near airport), Marriott, Hilton, Radisson (events), Aloft (value), Dusit Princess (boutique) | personal service, location, business and event facilities, breakfast, a calm base away from the noise |
| Pokhara | lakes, mountains, leisure, trekking gateway | The Pavilions Himalayas (eco-luxury), Tiger Mountain Lodge (regenerative), Temple Tree (spa), Hotel Barahi (lakeside), Waterfront, Fish Tail Lodge, Pokhara Grande | views, lakeside calm, spa and wellness, post-trek recovery, airport pickup, the landscape as the experience |
| Chitwan | jungle, wildlife, Tharu culture | Meghauli Serai a Taj Safari (the benchmark), Barahi Jungle Lodge (ethical naturalists), Kasara Resort (design, award winning), Tiger Tops, Tigerland, Sapana Village (value), and Soaltee Westend Resort Chitwan | wildlife access, expert naturalists, the river and jungle setting, authentic Tharu culture, sustainability, the soft landing after a trek |
| Nepalgunj | western business and regional hub | Soaltee Westend Premier is the clear leader, with few true five-star rivals | business travel, regional events, weddings, and the casino draw, not leisure tourism |

Notes that shape messaging:
- In Chitwan, elephant-back safaris are widely discouraged on welfare grounds and many lodges have stopped them. Lead with jeep safaris, guided walks, canoeing, the elephant breeding centre, and Tharu culture. Set honest sighting expectations.
- Nepal tourism is shifting toward a high-value, sustainable model rather than mass crowds. Sustainability and ethics are selling points now, not footnotes.
- Bharatpur airport serves Chitwan, Pokhara has its own airport, and helicopter transfers are a real luxury option between the three.

## The segments and how to message each

| Segment | What to sell |

| Leisure and vacation | the feeling and the setting. Calm, the view, the moment of arrival |
| Business and MICE, office events | reliability, meeting and banquet space, connectivity, location, seamless logistics |
| Weddings and celebrations | the venue as a backdrop and the team that makes the day effortless |
| Getaways and short breaks | the escape close to home, for the domestic and Indian market. A two-night reset |
| Family | space, pool, kid-friendly activities, safety |
| Wellness | the spa, the quiet, and recovery, especially post-trek in Pokhara |

## Design patterns from the world's hotel brands

Study how leading hotel brands present themselves, borrow the pattern underneath, and localize it with a real Nepali detail. Borrow the thinking, never the brand's signature.

The patterns that repeat: a strong sense of place, one signature ritual, service as the product written down, a wellbeing or sustainability thread, public space as experience, the arrival moment (the first sixty seconds), restraint and quiet luxury, the one unforgettable thing, and story over amenity list.

How to localize in Nepal: Newari craft and courtyards in Kathmandu, Tharu culture and the river in Chitwan, the lake and the Annapurna view in Pokhara. A tea or singing-bowl welcome, a tika greeting, a dawn call to the sunrise, a riverside evening fire.

## The idea-generation method

1. Name the property's real strength and the segment, from research and reviews.
2. Pick two or three patterns above that fit that strength.
3. Borrow the thinking, not the brand, and localize each with a specific Nepali detail.
4. Pressure-test each idea: is it true to the property, can it actually be delivered, is it ownable.
5. Output three distinct directions, each tied to a pattern, with the one unforgettable thing up front.

Hard rule: never copy a brand's signature or claim another's identity. Authenticity beats imitation of international spectacle every time.

## The trend patterns most hotels follow in 2026

Apply only the ones that fit the property and segment. Never chase every trend. Refresh through research, since platforms move.

- Short-form video first (reels, Shorts): one weekly short of the property and its setting.
- Authenticity over polish: film real staff, real guests, real light. Over-produced spots get skipped.
- UGC as the trust signal: a content nook, a branded hashtag, repost guest reels with credit.
- Micro and nano influencers: host a regional creator rather than a celebrity.
- Sell the feeling, not the room: lead with rest and reconnection, the calm Nepal does well.
- Direct booking over OTAs: a bookable bio link and a fast mobile site.
- Funnel content: discovery reels, behind-the-scenes trust, offer and testimonial posts to convert.
- Local destination content: roughly 60 percent local and destination, 30 percent property, 10 percent promotion.
- Social and AI search: keyword-rich captions, geotags, and a clear FAQ page.
- Reviews and social proof: prompt happy guests and reply personally to every review.
- Limited-time offers: festival, season, and weekend-getaway offers with a clear end date.
- Personalization through data: pre-arrival and post-stay emails tuned to past guests.
- DM and WhatsApp concierge: handle enquiries on Instagram and WhatsApp.

## Deliverables hotels need

**Online:** website copy (rooms, dining, spa, offers, events, weddings, MICE), OTA listings, Google Business Profile and Hotel Ads, email campaigns, social content and a monthly calendar, paid ads, blog and SEO, landing pages per offer, influencer and UGC briefs, chat templates, review responses, and visual briefs for the production crew.

**Offline:** brochure and fact sheet, in-room collateral (welcome card, directory, menus), signage and wayfinding, outdoor and display, wedding and MICE kits (pitch decks, floor plans, banquet menus), promo print, stationery, press kit, vouchers and loyalty, table and lift media, print ads, and trade materials.

Keep the voice consistent across online and offline, so the website, the reel, the in-room card, and the wedding deck all sound like the same property.

## How to use this knowledge

- Refresh before pitching. Re-check the property's own site and recent reviews, and update this base when things change.
- Ground claims in reality. Promise only what the property has and what reviews support.
- Match message to segment and city. The words that sell a Chitwan safari do not sell a Nepalgunj conference.
- Respect the local calendar. Dashain, Tihar, and Teej, plus wedding season and the spring and autumn trekking seasons, shape demand. Time campaigns to them.
