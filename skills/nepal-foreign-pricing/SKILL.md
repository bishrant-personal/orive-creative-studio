# Nepal and Foreign Pricing

How to price creative and studio work for two very different buyers: the local Nepal market and foreign clients. These are different products to different people. Apply judgment, not a calculator. No em dashes, ever.

## Core principles

- **Run two rate cards.** A local card in NPR for the Nepal market, and an international card in USD for foreign clients. They are different products to different buyers. Never quote your local number to a foreign client.
- **Price to the buyer's market, not your cost of living.** A foreign client's reference point is what the work costs where they are. International rates commonly run several times the local rate for the same scope.
- **Prefer value or project pricing over hourly.** Hourly caps the upside and invites scrutiny of how fast you work. Quote the outcome, not the hours.
- **Always anchor with three tiers.** Present good, better, best and let the high option set the frame. Lead every quote with a "starts from" figure.
- **Protect scope.** Fixed deliverables, an explicit revision count, a change-order clause, and a deposit. For foreign clients, take 50 percent up front.
- **Scope before you quote.** Never put a number on the table before a short discovery call. See the discovery prompts in research-playbook if a deeper scan is needed.
- **Local market reality.** Nepali buyers are more price sensitive. Bundle, phase the work, and offer a smaller starter engagement rather than discounting the headline rate.

## Payment rails out of Nepal

- **Receiving:** Wise and Payoneer are common for receiving foreign payments. Platforms like Deel suit ongoing retainer relationships. SWIFT wires fit larger sums.
- **Fees and limits:** each rail has its own fees and limits. Factor them into the quote so the net is what you expect.
- **Compliance:** Nepal has forex receipt rules under the central bank. Route the owner to a local accountant for compliance and tax. This is logistics guidance, not financial or legal advice.

## How to think about a number

1. Start from the outcome the client gets, not the time it takes you.
2. Set the floor from the owner's rate floor, never go below it.
3. Match the card to the client's market: NPR for local, USD for foreign.
4. Build three tiers so the middle one looks like the sensible choice.
5. Name the deposit and the revision limit before talk of discounts.
