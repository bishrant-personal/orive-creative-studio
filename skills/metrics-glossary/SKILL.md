# Metrics Glossary

Plain-language definitions for the numbers that matter, with a short "what to watch" note for each. Read numbers as a story about human behavior, not a spreadsheet. No em dashes, ever.

## The one rule about benchmarks

Good benchmarks vary by market, objective, and audience. A "good" number in one account is a bad one in another. Always compare against the account's own trend over time, not internet averages. When someone asks "is this good?", the honest answer is "compared to what this account usually does, here is the read."

## The metrics

- **CPM (cost per 1000 impressions).** What it costs to be seen 1000 times. Watch it to gauge how expensive the audience is to reach. Rising CPM with flat results often means audience fatigue or more competition.

- **CPC (cost per click).** What you pay for one click. Watch it with CTR. High CPC with low CTR usually means the creative is not earning the click.

- **CTR (click-through rate).** Share of people who saw it and clicked. The clearest read on whether the creative and the offer connect. A low CTR is almost always a creative or targeting problem, not a budget problem.

- **CPA or CPL (cost per acquisition or lead).** What one customer or lead costs. The number that ties spend to outcome. Watch it against the value of a customer. If CPA is below what a customer is worth, you can scale.

- **ROAS (return on ad spend).** Revenue divided by spend. Watch it as the headline efficiency number for sales campaigns. A ROAS below break-even means the campaign loses money even while it "works."

- **Reach.** How many distinct people saw it. Watch it against frequency. Reach without conversions points to a message or offer problem.

- **Frequency.** Average times each person saw it. Watch for fatigue. When frequency climbs and CTR falls, the audience has seen enough. Refresh the creative or widen the audience.

- **Engagement rate.** Share of viewers who interacted. Useful for organic and awareness. Watch it as an early signal before conversion data is thick enough to trust.

- **Video hook rate.** Share who watched past the first few seconds. The single best read on whether the opening works. A weak hook rate means fix the first 2 seconds before anything else.

- **Video hold rate.** Share who watched most or all of it. Watch it with hook rate. A strong hook but weak hold means the open promises something the rest does not deliver.

## How to read an account

1. Find the one metric that explains the others (often hook rate or CTR).
2. Compare it to this account's own recent trend.
3. Say what it means in plain words, then the likely cause.
4. Propose the smallest test that would settle the question.
