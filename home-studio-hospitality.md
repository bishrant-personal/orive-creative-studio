# Home Studio: Hotel and Hospitality Specialist

A companion to `home-studio-build-brief.md`. It adds one specialist plus a researched knowledge base for hotels, resorts, vacations, events, corporate and office events, getaways, and tourism at the Nepal level.

The knowledge is built from real properties and real guest reviews, not opinion. It is meant to be refreshed by research rather than assumed, because properties, rooms, and competitors change. The same house rules apply, including no em dashes, ever.

## Where it lives

To keep the repo tidy and avoid duplication, the full content is implemented in two files rather than repeated here:

- **The specialist:** `.claude/agents/hospitality.md`. Reached by everyday words (hotel, resort, stay, getaway, tourism, package, banquet, MICE, wedding venue, safari stay). The host announces it and carries the chosen voice. It grounds every claim in research and reviews and never invents a feature.
- **The knowledge base:** `.claude/skills/hospitality-knowledge/SKILL.md`. What guests in Nepal actually value, the Soaltee portfolio, the competitive map by city, the segments and how to message each, design patterns borrowed from the world's hotel brands, the idea-generation method, the 2026 trend patterns, and the full online and offline deliverable menu.

## How it fits Home Studio

This specialist joins the roster like the others. It leans on the `hospitality-knowledge` skill, reuses any existing property-scouting skill rather than duplicating it, and pulls in Copywriter, Content, Videographer, Cinematographer, and Brand through the host when a job needs words, social, film, or design. Its keywords are in the access map in `CLAUDE.md`.

## How to use the knowledge

- Refresh before pitching. Re-check the property's own site and recent reviews, and update the skill when things change.
- Ground claims in reality. Promise only what the property has and what reviews support.
- Match message to segment and city. The words that sell a Chitwan safari do not sell a Nepalgunj conference.
- Respect the local calendar. Dashain, Tihar, and Teej, plus wedding season and the spring and autumn trekking seasons, shape demand. Time campaigns to them.
